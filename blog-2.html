Amazing Benefits of
Thanksgiving

"Praise the LORD! Oh give thanks to the LORD, for he is good, for his steadfast love endures forever!"
- Psalm 106:1

We can see from the above scripture that we have a clear instruction to give thanks to God. And we can also see why - for he is good: for his mercy endureth forever. The Word of God makes us to know that God is good, and he does good things (Psalm 119:68). God is good by nature, He is compassionate, kind and knows how to care for His own people. If we understand this, then we can learn and know how to position ourselves to expect and receive good from the One Who is our Father. We would understand that we need to constantly live with gratitude in our hearts. The Psalmist said, "I will bless the LORD at all times; his praise shall continually be in my mouth" (Psalm 34:1 ESV). "At all times" means in every situation, at every moment, no matter the state or condition of my life or what I'm going through... I will bless Him, I will be grateful.
The Psalmist knew there are benefits attached to being thankful to God and some of them are highlighted below.

  • Thanksgiving Provokes Increases.

    "I have engraved you on the palms of my hands;
    your walls are continually before me..."
    would" - John 6:11    . This was a MIRACLE of INCREASE on the platform of thanksgiving. As people of faith in Christ, if we desire divine increase in our lives, we need to be thankful to God always.
  • Thanksgiving attracks God's blessings.
    In addition to increase, God blesses us when we are thankful to Him. "Let the peoples praise you, O God; let all the peoples praise you!The earth has yielded its increase; God, our God, shall bless us." - Psalm 67:5-6.
  • Thanksgiving moves God to perfect all our concerns.
    God knows what we need to live a good, satisfactory and productive lives. When we thank Him always for the things He has done for us, we encourage Him to do even more for us. After one of the ten lepers that was healed by Jesus saw that he was cleansed, he turned back glorifying God and fell down to GIVE JESUS THANKS. Jesus noted that just one out of the ten lepers returned to glorify God, and He said to him, "...Rise and go your way; your faith has made you well (whole)" - Luke 17:11-19. Emphasis mine.

    • Let's be thankful! If we want to see good in life, being grateful to God is a first step. If we want to be in a celebration mode continually, then we need to be thankful. As the Bible puts it succinctly, "All the days of the afflicted are evil, but the cheerful of heart has a continual feast" - Proverbs 15:15.
